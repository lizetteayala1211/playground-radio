A CATALOG FOR A LONG BLACK TOMORROW
Jaylen Strong Introducing Catalog For A Long Black Tomorrow.

ABOUT:

Jaylen Strong presents Playground Radio's Catalog For A Long Black Tomorrow. Every year at Playground Coffee Shop we organize an event to celebrate, uplift, and showcase Black artists and creatives in honor of Black History Month, but amid the COVID-19 pandemic, we opted to bring you all an accessible online virtual experience!

CFALBT has been curated with the thought that the artists involved work from what we believe is an archivist's mold. We believe the work they create is influenced by their Black cultural past, their intimate realities, and– what we also believe to be–a determination to maintain a legacy of a diverse, lively, and polylithic Blackness. Amid the fight against a white-supremacist society, they know, we know that Black legacy will extend far beyond our imaginations.

There will be a very long Black Tomorrow.

This event features performances from Caleb Giles, Starchild & The New Romantic, and Samuel Omare; two DJ sets from Cardamami and Travis Brown; and two interviews featuring Duneska Suannette of Guanábana and chef and farmer Sammi Gay. These sessions were all recorded and supported by Pirate at their Bushwick studio location by filmmaker Chad Murdock.

This event was also sponsored in part by Carhartt WIP and Oatly!

We present, Catalog For A Long Black Tomorrow. We hope you all enjoy it.
Goin' On a Ride with Starchild & The New Romantic

ABOUT:

Bryndon Cook, known as Starchild & the New Romantic, is someone we want to listen to forever.

Check out his set where he runs us through beautiful renditions from his catalog. Here, he illuminates us with his tone and language!

Sammi Gay Talks Preservation & Guidance.

ABOUT:

Trained as an urban planner and farmer, Sammi Gay is a kitchen-poet (a la Edwidge Danticat) from the Bronx whose cooking dances between the line of play and preservation.

Throughout our interview with her she makes metaphor from soil!

Samuel Omare Does It For The Hood.

ABOUT:

Playground stalwart Samuel Omare gives a moody and necessary performance in the glow of a crimson shadow.

The songs here are situated at the crossroads of the hands of age, growth, and a host of exterior factors producing the events of life.

Caleb Giles and His Meditations!

ABOUT:

Bronx rapper, producer, and multi-instrumentalist Caleb Giles gives us a reverent performance of his brand new record Meditations.

We hear all the transient reflections embedded deep into the production of this album live and direct in all its rawness.

Duneska Desires Clarity.

ABOUT:

Duneska Suannette is a Black-Dominicanx Artist, Educator, and Community Organizer.

In their practice Suannette examines, critiques, and rejects Latinidad – thinking through more expansive ways to relate. This being evident in the collective they founded Summer 2020, Guanábana, which centers Black folx with Antillean Ancestry.

Travis Brown Sits at the Helm of the Cockpit.

ABOUT:

Travis Brown is a multidisciplinary creative from Largo, MD, and host of‚ By Proxy‚ on Playground Radio.

Along with the radio show, Travis hosts an all-vinyl show on Twitch, Travis Plays...; freelances in graphic design, product design, and event planning; and enjoys conversations with friends old and new.

Anisa Jackson aka DJ Cardamami Gives Us A Groove!

ABOUT:

Playground Radio Resident DJ Anisa Jackson steps in the booth and gives us a taste of a deep groove! Check out their set that roams over Southwest Asian and North Africa sounds!

Under the moniker DJ Cardamami, Anisa Jackson began DJing in Oslo and has played for venues and radio stations everywhere from Seattle, New York, and London to Istanbul and Mérida.

They host a biweekly radio show with Playground Radio called Spicy Trax. and are the co-host of Yalla Yeehaw, a party featuring Southwest Asian, North African & South Asian sounds.
